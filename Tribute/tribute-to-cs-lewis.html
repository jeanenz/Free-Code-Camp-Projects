C.S. Lewis

Father of Narnia
The lion Aslan gazes into the face of his creator, C.S. Lewis, author of the Chronicles of Narnia.

The life and creations of C.S. Lewis:

  • 1898 Born in Belfast Ireland
  • 1908 Mother dies from cancer. Lewis is sent to boarding school
  • 1912 Abandons Christianity to become an atheist.
  • 1916 Receives a scholarship to Oxford.
  • 1917 Moves to England. Is shipped to Frances to fight in WWI where the horror of war confirms his atheism.
  • 1918 Wounded in battle, demobilized back to England, returns to Oxford.
  • 1926 Meets J.R.R. Tolkien who helps begin his conversion back to God.
  • 1929 "I gave in, and admitted that God was God, and knelt and prayed: perhaps, that night, the most dejected and reluctant convert in all England."
  • 1939 WWII. Took child evacuees from London and other cities
  • 1941-3 Gives talks on Christianity over the radio for the BBC
  • 1949-54 Writes the Chronicles of Narnia
  • 1956 Marries Joy Davidman
  • 1960 Joy Davidman dies of cancer
  • 1963 Dies of renal failure

"I believe in Christianity as I believe that the sun has risen: not only because I see it, but because by it I see everything else."

- C.S. Lewis
